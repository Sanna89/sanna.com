    ANXIETY

    Anxiety is a common response of our body causing stress or tension. Increased heart rate, sweating can be few signs of anxiety. It is normal to have anxiety every now and then but if someone has anxiety disorder then they might experience stress, extreme fear and worries very often like day to day. A person can have more than one anxiety disorder which can even lead to medical condition. Some of the worst symptoms of having anxiety disorder are nausea, not able to move or run, fear of losing control, choking. These can be treated with regular treatment. Some might need only a psychiatrist but in some cases medical treatment would be necessary. During the pandemic, anxiety has hit almost everyone in some way or other even without the knowledge of the person. Overthinking is also one of the symptoms of anxiety and here is a law which talks about overthinking. Its breathtaking to know more about this law. A gist of the origin and an example of Murphy’s law is given below for a quick and easier understanding. Murphy’s law “If anything can go wrong, it will anyway” – the more you fear something, there are high the chances of it happening. This isn’t any mysterious power or magic; in reality, we expect that things should work out in our favor but when it goes wrong, we search for reasons. ORIGIN- It was named after Captain Edward A Murphy who was an engineer working at an Air force project in the time 1949. One day after finding out the fault, he started to swear to his technician and said “If there is any way to do it wrong, he’ll find it”. The project contract manager had a list of laws and added this as MURPHY’S LAW. There was confusion about whether this is Murphy’s law or Sod’s law. Sod’s law was coined long (hundred years ago) before Murphy’s law. It is rather better to say Murphy’s law is a simpler version of Sod’s law. Every day the teacher comes late but today when I was late to class, the teacher was on time. This is an example of Murphy’s law.
